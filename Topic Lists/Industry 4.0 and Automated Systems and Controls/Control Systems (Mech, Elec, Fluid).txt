1. Mathematical Modeling of Dynamic Systems (15%)
   - First-order and second-order linear differential equations
   - Transfer functions and Laplace transforms
   - Modeling of mechanical systems using Newton’s laws, mass-spring-damper systems
   - Modeling of electrical systems using Ohm’s law and Kirchhoff’s laws, RLC circuits
   - Modeling of fluid systems using Bernoulli’s equation and conservation of mass, fluid flow in pipes and tanks
   - Derivation of system equations from physical laws and constitutive relations
   - Linearization of nonlinear systems around operating points

2. Time Domain Analysis (15%)
   - Step, ramp, and impulse responses of first- and second-order systems
   - Time response characteristics: rise time, settling time, overshoot, and steady-state error
   - Stability analysis for addressing chattering
   - Stability analysis using root locus method, construction of root locus plots
   - Effect of damping ratio and natural frequency on time response
   - Analysis of transient and steady-state behavior in mechanical, electrical, and fluid systems

3. Frequency Domain Analysis (15%)
   - Frequency response of first- and second-order systems, magnitude and phase
   - Bode plots: magnitude and phase plots, asymptotic approximations, gain and phase crossover frequencies
   - Nyquist plots: construction, stability via encirclement of critical point, Nyquist stability criterion
   - Root locus in frequency domain context, relationship to frequency response
   - Gain and phase margins: definition, computation, and interpretation for stability
   - Use of frequency response for system performance evaluation and controller design

4. Feedback Control Systems (15%)
   - Open-loop vs. closed-loop systems: advantages, limitations, and applications
   - Feedback principles: negative feedback, error signal generation, feedback path
   - Block diagram representation of feedback systems
   - Closed-loop transfer function derivation
   - Performance metrics in closed-loop systems: steady-state error, sensitivity, robustness
   - Design of PID controllers: proportional (P), integral (I), and derivative (D) terms
   - Tuning methods for PID controllers: Ziegler-Nichols, Cohen-Coon, and trial-and-error
   - Effect of each PID term on system response: overshoot, stability, steady-state error

5. System Identification and Parameter Estimation
   - Experimental data collection: sensor calibration, actuator response, noise considerations
   - Use of sensors and actuators in real-world physical systems, temperature, pressure, position, velocity
   - Model fitting techniques: parametric and non-parametric models
   - Parameter estimation methods: least squares estimation, maximum likelihood estimation
   - Validation of identified models: residual analysis, model accuracy, generalization
   - Application of system identification in adaptive control and real-time monitoring

6. Digital Control Systems
   - Discrete-time systems: difference equations and sampling process
   - Sampling theory: Nyquist rate, aliasing, hold circuits, zero-order and first-order
   - Z-transform: definition, properties, and application to discrete-time system analysis
   - Digital representation of continuous-time systems via sampling
   - Digital PID controller design and implementation
   - Implementation of digital controllers in embedded systems and IoT platforms
   - Real-time execution and timing considerations in digital control loops

7. Cyber-Physical Systems
   - Interactions between physical processes and digital control loops
   - Real-time data processing: latency, bandwidth, and system responsiveness
   - Integration of digital control with physical hardware in production environments
   - Fault detection mechanisms: anomaly detection, threshold-based monitoring
   - Adaptive control strategies in dynamic environments
   - Role of cyber-physical systems in smart manufacturing and industrial automation

8. Applications in Smart Manufacturing
   - Control of robotic arms: position, velocity, and trajectory control
   - Control of fluid flow in pipelines: pressure regulation, flow rate control
   - Control of electrical circuits: voltage, current, and power regulation
   - Integration with IoT sensors and real-time monitoring platforms
   - Case studies: temperature control in industrial processes, pressure regulation in fluid systems, motion control in automated systems
   - Implementation of control logic in embedded systems and PLCs

9. Design and Implementation Projects
   - Hands-on design of a control system for a mechanical, electrical, or fluid system
   - Use of simulation tools MATLAB/Simulink and LabVIEW for modeling, simulation, and controller design
   - Development of a complete control system from problem definition to implementation
   - Prototype testing: physical setup, data acquisition, performance evaluation
   - Performance metrics: accuracy, response time, robustness, and stability
   - Documentation and presentation of results including error analysis and improvement strategies
