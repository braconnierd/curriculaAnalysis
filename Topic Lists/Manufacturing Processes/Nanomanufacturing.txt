1. Fundamentals of Nanomanufacturing (20%)
   - Definition and scope of nanomanufacturing  
   - Scale and precision requirements at nanoscale:  
     - Sub-100 nm feature resolution  
     - Atomic-level control  
   - Surface effects:  
     - Surface tension  
     - Surface energy  
     - van der Waals forces  
   - Thermal management:  
     - Heat dissipation at nanoscale  
     - Localized heating  
   - Defect control:  
     - Point defects  
     - Dislocations  
     - Contamination  

2. Nanomaterials and Their Fabrication (20%)
   - Types of nanomaterials:  
     - Nanoparticles (spherical, rod-shaped)  
     - Nanowires (1D)  
     - Nanotubes (carbon nanotubes, semiconductor nanotubes)  
     - Nanolayers (thin films, nanocoatings)  
   - Synthesis methods:  
     - Chemical vapor deposition (CVD):  
       - Plasma-enhanced CVD (PECVD)  
       - Thermal CVD  
     - Sol-gel process:  
       - Sol formation  
       - Gelation  
       - Drying  
       - Thermal treatment  
     - Hydrothermal synthesis:  
       - Temperature control  
       - Pressure control  
       - Solvent control  
     - Microwave-assisted synthesis  
     - Laser ablation  
     - Precipitation  
     - Self-assembly  
   - Properties and performance metrics:  
     - Surface area-to-volume ratio  
     - Quantum confinement effects  
     - Optical properties (plasmon resonance)  
     - Electrical conductivity  
     - Mechanical strength  
     - Thermal stability  

3. Nanofabrication Processes (20%)
   - Lithography:  
     - Photolithography:  
       - UV, deep UV, and extreme UV (EUV)  
       - Resolution limits  
       - Photoresist chemistry  
     - Electron beam lithography (e-beam):  
       - Scanning electron beam  
       - Dose control  
       - Pattern fidelity  
       - Resist materials  
   - Etching and deposition techniques:  
     - Plasma etching:  
       - Dry etching (reactive ion etching, RIE)  
       - Wet etching  
       - Etch selectivity and anisotropy  
     - Atomic layer deposition (ALD):  
       - Self-limiting reactions  
       - Layer thickness control  
       - Uniformity  
       - Deposition rate  
   - Thin film and layer-by-layer assembly:  
     - Langmuir-Blodgett (LB) films  
     - Layer-by-layer (LbL) self-assembly:  
       - Polyelectrolyte multilayers  
       - Biomolecular layering  
       - Interfacial control  

4. Additive Manufacturing at Nanoscale
   - Nanoparticle-based 3D printing:  
     - Inkjet-based nanoparticle printing  
     - Laser-assisted nanoparticle deposition  
     - Suspension-based extrusion with nanoparticle dispersion  
   - Layer-by-layer additive techniques:  
     - Sequential deposition with controlled interfacial bonding  
     - Self-assembly in solution-phase layering  
   - Process parameters and control:  
     - Particle size distribution  
     - Dispersion stability  
     - Viscosity  
     - Layer thickness  
     - Thermal and mechanical stability  
     - Real-time monitoring of deposition dynamics  

5. Design for Nanomanufacturing
   - Topology optimization:  
     - Generative design algorithms  
     - Finite element constraints  
     - Integration of mechanical and thermal constraints  
   - Dimensional tolerances and feature scaling:  
     - Feature size limits  
     - Dimensional accuracy  
     - Nanoscale deviation  
     - Tolerance stack-up effects  
   - Integration of design and manufacturability:  
     - Design for assembly (DFA)  
     - Design for maintenance (DFM)  
     - Simulation-driven manufacturability prediction  

6. Simulation and Visualization Tools
   - Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD):  
     - Fluid flow in micro/nano channels  
     - Thermal gradients and heat transfer  
   - Finite Element Method (FEM):  
     - Stress and strain distribution  
     - Thermal expansion  
     - Mechanical response  
   - Digital twins:  
     - Real-time process replication  
     - Predictive analytics for failure modes  
   - Real-time monitoring and modeling:  
     - Sensor integration and machine learning  
     - 3D visualization and time-lapse simulation  

7. Sustainability and Green Manufacturing
   - Energy efficiency:  
     - Energy use in CVD, lithography, etching, deposition  
     - Process cycle optimization  
   - Waste reduction and environmental impact:  
     - Emissions (volatile organic compounds)  
     - Byproduct management (solvents, residues)  
     - Life cycle assessment (LCA)  
   - Sustainable materials:  
     - Biodegradable polymers  
     - Non-toxic precursors  
     - Recyclable resists
