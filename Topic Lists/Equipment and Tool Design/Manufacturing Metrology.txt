1. Fundamentals of Gage Design (20%)
   - Definition and purpose of gages  
   - Types of gages (go/no-go, limit, comparative)  
   - Tolerance stack-up and its impact on gage design  
   - Role of gages in quality control and inspection  

2. Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing (GD&T) (20%)  
   - Principles of GD&T (datum, feature control frames)  
   - Application of GD&T in gage design for part verification  
   - Interpreting engineering drawings with GD&T  

3. Material Selection and Properties (20%)
   - Mechanical properties of gage materials (hardness, wear resistance, yield strength, fatigue resistance)  
   - Selection criteria based on application (temperature, load, environmental exposure, corrosion resistance)  
   - Common materials (steel, stainless steel, hardened alloys, ceramics, polymers)  
   - Trade-offs between material properties and cost  

4. Gage Function and Measurement Principles  
   - Types of measurement (linear, diameter, depth, profile, angle, surface)  
   - Sensitivity, resolution, and repeatability in measurement systems  
   - Calibration and traceability requirements (traceability to national standards)  
   - Influence of measurement principle on gage design (contact vs. non-contact, mechanical vs. optical)  

5. Design for Manufacturing and Assembly (DFMA)
   - Minimizing design complexity and cost (reducing part count, simplifying geometry)  
   - Integration with manufacturing processes (CNC machining, injection molding, casting, turning)  
   - Ease of maintenance and repair (accessibility, modularity, replaceability)  
   - Design for assembly (DFA) principles in gage construction  

6. Digital Tools and CAD/CAE/CAM Integration  
   - Using CAD to model gage parts (parametric modeling, toleranced design, feature creation)  
   - CAE for simulation of measurement accuracy (finite element analysis of deformation, thermal effects, contact stress)  
   - CAM for generating toolpaths and manufacturing instructions (tool selection, cutting parameters, machine constraints)  
   - Digital workflow integration (from concept to production, including virtual prototyping)  

7. Error Analysis and Uncertainty  
   - Sources of measurement error (systematic, random, environmental, operator-induced)  
   - Statistical analysis of gage performance (mean, standard deviation, bias, repeatability)  
   - Uncertainty propagation in measurement chains (Monte Carlo simulation, error propagation models)  
   - Evaluation of gage performance under real-world conditions  

8. Case Studies in Gage Design  
   - Real-world examples from fiber extrusion and mini-mill systems  
   - Design challenges and solutions in small-scale manufacturing (thermal expansion, material variability, precision requirements)  
   - Comparative analysis of gage designs across different production environments  
   - Lessons learned from failure modes and iterative improvements  

9. Validation and Testing Protocols  
   - Laboratory testing of gages (static and dynamic performance tests)  
   - Verification against reference standards (master gages, calibrated instruments)  
   - Documentation and reporting standards (test records, traceability logs, compliance reports)  
   - Acceptance criteria and performance benchmarks  

10. Regulatory and Industry Standards  
   - ISO 2768 (tolerance classification for general tolerances)  
   - ASME B89.4.3 (gaging and measurement standards)  
   - ISO 9001 (quality management systems and inspection requirements)  
   - ANSI/ASME B89.1 (gaging systems and their use)  
   - Compliance in design and certification (audit readiness, documentation, traceability)  
   - Application of standards to specific gage types and measurement functions
