1. Design for Manufacturing (DFM) (30%)
   - Feature identification and simplification  
   - Material selection for manufacturability  
   - Tolerancing and geometric dimensioning (GD&T)  
   - Surface finish and machining considerations  
   - Tooling and fixture compatibility  
   - Process flow integration (CNC, injection molding)  

2. Design for Assembly (DFA) (30%)
   - Modularity and standardization  
   - Interchangeability and alignment  
   - Minimizing assembly steps and tools  
   - Accessibility and ergonomics  

3. Design for Maintenance (DFMnt)  
   - Accessibility of components  
   - Diagnostic and service points  
   - Replaceable parts and modular design  
   - Condition monitoring integration  

4. Design for Remanufacturing (DFR)  
   - Disassembly design and part identification  
   - Condition assessment and repairability  
   - Part standardization and labeling  
   - Lifecycle data integration  

5. Design for Recycling (DFRc)  
   - Material recyclability and end-of-life design  
   - Separability of materials  
   - Avoidance of hazardous substances  
   - Packaging and labeling for recovery  

6. Design for Sustainability (DFS)  
   - Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) integration  
   - Energy efficiency and emissions  
   - Circular economy principles  
   - Environmental impact modeling  

7. Digital Twin and Simulation-Based Design  
   - Real-time monitoring and feedback  
   - Virtual prototyping for manufacturing processes  
   - Digital twin integration in design phases  

8. Experiential and Hands-On Learning  
   - Learning Factory environments  
   - CAD/CAE/CAM integration  
   - CNC and additive manufacturing labs  

9. Industry 4.0 and 5.0 Integration  
   - IoT and data-driven design  
   - Human-centric design in smart systems  
   - Sustainability and digital transformation  

10. Cross-Curricular and Entrepreneurial Design  
   - Business-technical integration  
   - New product development (NPD)  
   - Value creation and innovation
