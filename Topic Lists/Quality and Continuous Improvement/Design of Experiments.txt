1. Experimental Design Fundamentals  
   - Types of experiments (completely randomized, factorial, block, repeated measures)  
   - Principles of randomization, replication, and blocking  
   - Design matrices and factor levels  

2. Variance and Error Analysis  
   - Sources of variation in manufacturing processes  
   - Measurement error and precision  
   - Residual analysis and normality testing  

3. Statistical Process Control (SPC)  
   - X-bar and R charts construction and interpretation  
   - Control limits (UCL, LCL) calculation  
   - Process stability and capability (Cp, Cpk)  
   - Out-of-control signal identification (non-random patterns)  

4. Design of Experiments (DOE) in Manufacturing  
   - Application of DOE to optimize cutting, cutting speed, material thickness  
   - Taguchi methods and orthogonal arrays  
   - Response surface methodology (RSM) for process optimization  

5. Quality Management and Process Improvement  
   - PDCA cycle integration (Plan-Do-Check-Act)  
   - Root cause analysis (5 Whys, Fishbone)  
   - Failure mode and effects analysis (FMEA)  

6. Experimental Tools and Tools Integration  
   - Use of jigs, templates, and measurement devices (rulers, protractors, pencils)  
   - Standardization of measurement points (top edge, diagonal)  
   - Sample selection and systematic sampling (four squares per group)  

7. Data Collection and Analysis in Real-World Settings  
   - Real-time data entry in Excel templates  
   - Error handling and data validation  
   - Visualization of control charts and process trends  

8. Process Capability and Performance Metrics  
   - Calculation of Cp and Cpk  
   - Interpretation of capability indices  
   - Comparison of target vs. specification limits  

9. Iterative Design and Feedback Loops  
   - Adjusting process parameters based on trial results  
   - Documenting changes and justifying revisions  
   - Transition from trial to full-scale production  

10. Communication of Findings and Recommendations  
   - Executive summary for management  
   - Final report structure (title, findings, changes, analysis, recommendations)
