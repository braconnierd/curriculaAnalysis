1. Tool Material Properties and Selection (20%)

   - Environmental and cost considerations
   - Coatings  

2. Tool Geometry and Its Impact on Cutting Performance (20%)
   - Cutting edge angle (rake angle, clearance angle)  
   - Effect of geometry on chip formation and tool life  
   - Tool face and flank wear mechanisms  
   - Optimization of geometry for specific materials  

3. Cutting Forces and Tool Dynamics (20%)
   - Force modeling in turning, milling, drilling  
   - Influence of feed rate, depth of cut, and speed  
   - Vibration and chatter analysis  
   - Dynamic stability and tool deflection  

4. Tool Life Prediction and Wear Models  
   - Abrasive and thermal wear mechanisms  
   - Use of Taylor’s tool life equation  
   - Empirical and predictive models for tool life  
   - Influence of coolant and cutting parameters  

5. Material Compatibility and Tool Selection  
   - Selection based on workpiece material (steel, aluminum, composites)

   - Effect of material microstructure and other parameters on surface finish

6. Tool Design for Specific Machining Operations

   - Tool holder and shank design considerations  
   - Integration with CNC systems 

7. Digital Tool Design and Simulation
   - CAD for tool design

   - Use of finite element analysis (FEA) for stress and deformation  

8. Safety, Maintenance, and Tool Handling  
   - Tool storage, inspection, and replacement protocols

   - Preventive maintenance schedules  

9. Case Studies in Tool Selection  
   - Real-world industrial scenarios

10. Evaluation of Tool Performance Metrics
