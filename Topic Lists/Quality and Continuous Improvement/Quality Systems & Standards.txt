1. Foundations of Quality Systems  
   - Definition and evolution of quality systems  
   - Core principles of quality (customer focus, leadership, engagement, decision-making, involvement)  
   - Historical development of quality: Shewhart, Deming, Juran, Crosby, Taylor, and modern evolution  
   - Quality vs. conformance: distinction between quality as a state of excellence and conformance to specifications  

2. Quality Standards and Frameworks  
   - ISO 9001: core structure (context, leadership, planning, support, operation, performance evaluation, improvement), clause-by-clause breakdown, and implementation roadmap  
   - ISO 13485: applicability to medical devices, specific requirements for design, production, and quality management in healthcare environments  
   - ISO 14001: environmental management systems and integration with quality systems  
   - Application of quality standards in engineering design and manufacturing: from concept to production, including documentation, traceability, and compliance  

3. Statistical Process Control (SPC)  
   - X-bar and R charts: data collection, construction, interpretation, and use in monitoring process stability  
   - Control limits calculation: upper control limit (UCL) and lower control limit (LCL) derivation using sample means and ranges  
   - Process capability indices: Cp (potential capability), Cpk (actual capability), interpretation of values, and process performance assessment  
   - Types of variation: common cause vs. special cause variation, identification, and response strategies  
   - Real-time monitoring applications: use of SPC in manufacturing environments ("Manufacturing Squares" exercise) to demonstrate process control and decision-making  

4. Design for Quality (DFQ) and Quality Function Deployment (QFD)  
   - QFD principles: the House of Quality, customer requirement translation, and prioritization of features  
   - Integration of QFD with product and process design: from concept to design specifications  
   - Use of AI tools (ChatGPT) in QFD for engineering design: automation of customer requirement mapping, feature prioritization, and design feedback  
   - DFQ principles: minimizing defects through design, cost, and process integration  

5. Lean Manufacturing and Continuous Improvement  
   - Lean principles: value stream mapping, identification and elimination of waste (Muda, Muri, Muri), flow optimization  
   - PDCA cycle (Plan-Do-Check-Act) as a structured learning and improvement framework  
   - Role of Problem-Based Learning (PBL) in lean education: fostering critical thinking, real-world problem solving, and team-based decision making   
   - Vertical integration of quality concepts across engineering curricula: alignment of quality principles in design, manufacturing, and operations courses

6. Quality Tools and Techniques  
   - Flowcharts: purpose, construction, and use in process mapping and analysis  
   - Check sheets: data collection tools for defect tracking and process monitoring  
   - Pareto analysis: identifying the most significant issues using the 80/20 rule and prioritization  
   - Root cause analysis: 5 Whys technique, Fishbone (Ishikawa) diagram, and application in failure investigation  
   - Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA): structure (failure mode, severity, occurrence, detection), risk priority number (RPN), and implementation in design and process stages  

7. Quality in Engineering Education and Professional Practice  
   - Competency development in engineering graduates: expected competencies in quality, problem solving, and decision making 
   - Role of hands-on activities ("Manufacturing Squares") in skill acquisition: experiential learning, process understanding, and real-world application  
   - Gamification in teaching SPC: use of game-based learning to enhance engagement, retention, and performance  
   - Integration of quality education into mechanical engineering curricula: context of Mechanics 4.0 and digital transformation

8. Implementation and Evaluation of Quality Systems  
   - Process design and validation: ensuring processes meet quality objectives and are robust under variation  
   - Monitoring and auditing quality systems: internal audits, audit checklists, and compliance verification  
   - Feedback loops and iterative improvement: mechanisms for collecting data, identifying gaps, and implementing corrective actions  
   - Evaluation metrics: key performance indicators (KPIs), non-conformance rates, customer satisfaction, and system effectiveness
