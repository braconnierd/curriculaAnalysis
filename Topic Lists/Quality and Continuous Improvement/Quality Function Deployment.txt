1. Customer Requirements Elicitation  
   - Identifying customer needs through interviews, surveys, and market analysis  
   - Translating verbal and implicit needs into measurable, prioritized requirements  
   - Using VOC (Voice of the Customer) tools to capture functional and emotional needs  

2. Quality Function Deployment (QFD) Matrix Construction  
   - Building the House of Quality (HoQ) with customer requirements as rows and technical characteristics as columns  
   - Assigning weights and priorities to customer requirements  
   - Mapping technical features to customer needs using correlation and impact matrices  
   - Validating HoQ consistency through cross-functional alignment and stakeholder review  

3. Technical Translation and Design Specification  
   - Converting customer requirements into engineering specifications  
   - Defining design parameters, tolerances, and performance metrics  
   - Linking design outputs to process inputs using functional specifications  
   - Establishing design robustness and failure modes through failure mode and effects analysis (FMEA) integration  

4. Process Design and Feasibility Analysis  
   - Developing process flow diagrams (PFDs) and value stream maps  
   - Evaluating process feasibility using cost, time, and resource constraints  
   - Identifying potential bottlenecks and non-value-added steps  
   - Conducting a feasibility assessment for scalability and adaptability under varying operational conditions  

5. Cross-Functional Team Integration  
   - Establishing roles and responsibilities across design, manufacturing, and operations  
   - Facilitating communication between engineering, production, and quality teams  
   - Using collaborative tools (shared digital dashboards) for real-time updates  
   - Designing team workflows to ensure alignment of goals and shared accountability  

6. Validation and Feedback Loops  
   - Testing QFD outputs with prototypes or pilot runs  
   - Collecting feedback from users and stakeholders  
   - Iteratively refining the HoQ based on real-world performance  
   - Documenting validation outcomes and incorporating lessons learned into next-phase design  

7. Integration with Statistical Process Control (SPC)  
   - Using SPC tools (X-bar/R charts, capability analysis) to monitor process stability  
   - Linking QFD outputs to SPC control limits and process capability indices (Cp, Cpk)  
   - Ensuring design specifications are achievable under process variation  
   - Performing process capability studies to validate design-to-process alignment  

8. Implementation and Continuous Improvement  
   - Developing a roadmap for full-scale production  
   - Embedding QFD into organizational culture  
   - Applying PDCA (Plan-Do-Check-Act) cycles for ongoing improvement  
   - Evaluating long-term performance metrics against initial QFD objectives  
   - Conducting periodic QFD reviews to adapt to market, technological, and customer shifts
