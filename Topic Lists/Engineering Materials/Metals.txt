1. Foundational Science of Metals (20%)
    - Atomic structure and bonding in metals  
    - Crystal structures (FCC, BCC, HCP) and their properties  
    - Defects in metals (dislocations, vacancies, grain boundaries)  
    - Phase diagrams (binary, ternary) and equilibrium states  
    - Thermodynamics of phase transformations  

2. Mechanical Behavior of Metals (20%)
    - Stress-strain relationships and elastic/plastic deformation  
    - Yield strength, tensile strength, ductility  
    - Hardening mechanisms (work hardening, strain hardening)  
    - Fatigue and fracture mechanics  
    - Creep and stress relaxation under high temperature  

3. Processing and Fabrication Techniques (20%)
    - Casting (sand, die, investment)  
    - Rolling, extrusion, forging  
    - Welding (arc, TIG, MIG) and joining processes  
    - Heat treatment (annealing, quenching, tempering)  
    - Surface treatments (galvanizing, coating, nitriding)  

4. Material Selection and Design  
    - Properties vs. applications (strength, corrosion, cost)  
    - Selection criteria for structural and functional components  
    - Life cycle analysis of metal components  

5. Quality Control and Testing  
    - Non-destructive testing (ultrasonic, X-ray, eddy current)  
    - Tensile, hardness, impact testing  
    - Microstructural analysis (optical, SEM)  

6. Sustainability and Environmental Impact  
    - Recycling of metals and energy efficiency in processing  
    - Emissions and waste management in metal production  
    - Lifecycle environmental footprint  

7. Industry Applications and Case Studies  
    - Real-world examples in automotive, aerospace, construction  
    - Failure analysis in industrial metal components  
    - Integration of metals in smart manufacturing systems  

8. Pedagogical Implementation  
    - Hands-on lab sessions with metal samples and tools  
    - Video-based instruction for processes (welding, heat treatment)  
    - Use of simulations and virtual labs for phase diagrams and deformation  

9. Skill Development  
    - Problem-solving in material selection  
    - Critical thinking in failure analysis  
    - Communication of technical findings
