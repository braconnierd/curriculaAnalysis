1. Fundamentals of Natural Materials (20%)
   - Definition and classification of natural materials (biopolymers, minerals, biomaterials)  
   - Origin and formation processes (geological, biological)  
   - Physical and chemical properties (density, porosity, thermal conductivity, elasticity, moisture content)  
   - Sustainability metrics (carbon footprint, renewability, energy payback time, environmental impact per unit mass)  

2. Structure-Property Relationships in Natural Materials  
   - Hierarchical organization (molecular to nano-scale to macroscopic)  
   - Role of microstructure in mechanical behavior (tensile strength, fracture toughness, fatigue resistance)  
   - Case studies: bone (collagen and hydroxyapatite), wood (cellulose, hemicellulose, lignin), coral (calcite architecture), shell (biomineralized layers)  
   - Biomineralization and self-assembly mechanisms (molecular templating, ion transport, protein-mediated nucleation)  

3. Natural Material Processing and Extraction(20%) 
   - Sustainable harvesting and collection methods (selective harvesting, low-impact collection, community-based practices)  
   - Environmental impact assessment of extraction (biodiversity loss, soil erosion, water pollution)  
   - Energy efficiency in processing (dry processing, low-temperature methods, minimal chemical use)  
   - Preservation of ecological integrity during material sourcing  

4. Design for Natural Materials  (20%)
   - Biomimicry principles and design frameworks (biomimetic design, nature-inspired innovation)  
   - Optimization of form, function, and performance (load distribution, weight efficiency, adaptability)  
   - Integration with sustainable design (green design, zero-waste design, regenerative design)  
   - Design for disassembly and end-of-life compatibility  

5. Additive Manufacturing of Natural Materials  
   - 3D printing of biopolymers (PLA from cornstarch), natural composites (cellulose-based), and bio-based materials  
   - Challenges in material flow, layer adhesion, dimensional stability, and post-processing  
   - Scalability and reproducibility of natural material-based 3D printing  
   - Case studies from academic and industrial settings (printed biodegradable scaffolds, natural fiber composites)  

6. Lifecycle Assessment and Circular Economy  
   - Full lifecycle analysis (extraction, processing, use, end-of-life)  
   - Reusability, recyclability, and biodegradability of natural materials  
   - Life cycle impact assessment (LCA) metrics (carbon footprint, water use, land use)  
   - Integration with circular design principles (closed-loop systems, material recovery, waste-to-resource)    

7. Hands-on Laboratory and Field Experience  
   - Material testing (tensile, compression, flexural), sample preparation, microstructural analysis (microscopy, SEM)  
   - Observation of natural materials in ecosystems (forests, wetlands, coral reefs)  
   - Use of CAD/CAE tools for design, simulation, and prototyping of natural material-based structures  

8. Industry-University Collaboration and Real-World Applications  
   - Use of natural materials in IoT-enabled devices, sustainable construction  
   - Partnerships with sustainable tech firms (bio-based material startups, green engineering firms)  
   - Development of pilot projects (natural material-based products for urban infrastructure, fashion, or packaging)  
   - Knowledge transfer and innovation pipelines between academic research and industrial implementation  
   - Evaluation of design innovation, sustainability, and technical proficiency
