1. Fundamentals of Capacity Planning (20%)
   - Definition and scope of capacity planning  
   - Key performance indicators (KPIs) in capacity planning  
   - Relationship between capacity and throughput  
   - Demand forecasting methods (time series, regression)  

2. Capacity Requirements Planning (CRP) and Master Production Scheduling (MPS) (20%)
   - CRP process and workflow  
   - Integration of MPS with capacity constraints  
   - Workload balancing and resource allocation  
   - Capacity planning in multi-stage and multi-product environments  

3. Capacity Utilization and Bottleneck Analysis (20%)
   - Identification of bottleneck operations  
   - Utilization metrics and efficiency ratios (utilization rate, efficiency index)  
   - Sensitivity analysis of capacity changes under varying load conditions  
   - Impact of bottlenecks on overall system throughput and lead time  

4. Simulation-Based Capacity Design  
   - Introduction to discrete-event simulation  
   - Modeling manufacturing flows and capacity constraints  
   - Validation and scenario testing in metal industry contexts  
   - Use of simulation to evaluate capacity trade-offs under different production scenarios  

5. Sustainable Capacity Planning   
   - Environmental impact of capacity decisions (resource consumption, waste generation)  
   - Energy efficiency and emissions in capacity design (power usage per unit output)  
   - Lifecycle assessment integration (LCA) in capacity planning decisions  
   - Sustainable capacity optimization under environmental regulations and constraints  

6. Additive Manufacturing and Capacity Flexibility 
   - Role of 3D printing in dynamic capacity adjustment (on-demand, modular production)  
   - Design for manufacturability and capacity scalability in additive processes  
   - Case studies on rapid prototyping and on-demand production in small-batch and custom manufacturing  
   - Capacity flexibility in response to changing product demand and design iterations  

7. Learning Factory Environment and Capacity Development
   - Maintenance planning as a capacity management component (preventive and corrective maintenance)   
   - Real-time monitoring and adaptive capacity response in simulated production environments  

8. Granularity and Complexity in Capacity Design  
   - Granularity levels in manufacturing systems (machine, workstation, process, facility)  
   - Complexity modeling of capacity configurations (interdependencies, variability, uncertainty)  
   - Scalability and adaptability across production scales (from pilot to mass production)  
   - Trade-offs between granularity and system complexity in capacity design  

9. Capacity Planning in Dynamic and Uncertain Environments 
   - Resilience and adaptability of capacity plans under external shocks  
   - Response to demand volatility and supply disruptions (supplier failure, demand spikes)  
   - Scenario planning and risk mitigation strategies (buffer capacity, diversification)  
   - Real-time capacity adjustment mechanisms in volatile markets  

10. Evaluation and Validation of Capacity Plans  
   - Performance benchmarking against KPIs (utilization rate, throughput, cycle time)  
   - Feedback integration from real-world operations (production data, performance logs)  
   - Continuous improvement cycles (PDCA, Kaizen) in capacity planning  
   - Validation of simulation outputs against actual performance metrics and field data
