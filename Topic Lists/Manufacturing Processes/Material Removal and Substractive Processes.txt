1. Fundamentals of Material Removal  
   - Definition and classification of material removal processes  
   - Energy transfer mechanisms in machining  
   - Tool-workpiece interaction dynamics  
   - Cutting forces and chip formation  

2. Cutting Tools and Tool Geometry (20%)
   - Tool materials (high-speed steel, carbides, ceramics)  
   - Tool life and wear mechanisms  
   - Tool angles (cutting edge, rake, clearance)  
   - Tool coatings and surface treatments  

3. Machining Processes (20%)
   - Turning (external and internal)  
   - Milling (face, end, slot)  
   - Drilling
   - Boring  
   - Grinding (surface, cylindrical, centerless)  
   - Shaping
   - Planing 

4. Process Parameters and Optimization (20%)
   - Speed, feed, depth of cut  
   - Cutting fluid selection and function  
   - Thermal and vibration analysis  
   - Optimization using simulation and design of experiments  

5. Tool Wear and Surface Integrity  
   - Wear models (abrasive, adhesive, diffusion)  
   - Surface roughness and residual stress  
   - Microstructural changes during machining  

6. Numerical Control (CAM) and Design for Manufacturability  
   - CAM software fundamentals (CNC programming)  
   - G-code and toolpath generation  
   - DFM principles (tolerance, feature size, assembly)  

7. Process Selection and Decision Models 
   - Criteria for selecting machining processes  
   - Cost, accuracy, material, and environmental trade-offs  
   - Decision trees and process flow diagrams
