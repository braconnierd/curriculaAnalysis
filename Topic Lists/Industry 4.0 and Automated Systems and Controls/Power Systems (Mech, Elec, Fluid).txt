1. Fundamentals of Power Generation (20%)  
   - Energy conversion principles (mechanical to electrical)  
   - Thermodynamic cycles (Rankine, Brayton)  
   - Prime movers (steam, gas turbines)  
   - Generator operation and electromagnetic induction  
   - Power plant layout and system integration  

2. Power Transmission and Distribution 
   - AC/DC transmission systems  
   - Voltage transformation and transformer design  
   - Grid stability and reactive power management  
   - Line losses and impedance modeling  
   - Protection schemes (overcurrent, differential)  

3. Mechanical Systems in Power Plants  
   - Turbine dynamics and shaft alignment  
   - Bearing and seal systems  
   - Vibration analysis and fault detection  
   - Mechanical efficiency and wear modeling  

4. Fluid Dynamics in Power Systems (20%)
   - Fluid flow in turbines and pumps  
   - Pressure drop and flow rate modeling  
   - Hydraulic systems in auxiliary operations  
   - Fluid-structure interaction in rotating systems  

5. Control Systems in Power Systems  (20%)
   - Feedback control in generators and turbines  
   - PID controllers and stability margins  
   - Real-time monitoring and sensor integration  
   - Cyber-physical control loops   

6. IoT Integration for Power System Monitoring 
   - Sensor deployment in turbines, generators, and pipelines  
   - Real-time data acquisition and edge computing  
   - Predictive maintenance using anomaly detection  
   - Cloud-based dashboards and data analytics 

7. Interdisciplinary System Integration  
   - Synergistic design of mechanical, electrical, and fluid components  
   - System-level performance optimization  
   - Fault propagation modeling across subsystems  
   - Scalable factory-level integration   

8. Smart Manufacturing and Power System Operations  
   - Digital twins for power plant simulation  
   - Human-machine interface (HMI) design  
   - Workforce skill alignment with smart manufacturing   
   - Skill gap mitigation through hands-on labs
