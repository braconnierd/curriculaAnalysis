1. Design Thinking Foundations 
   - Origins and evolution of design thinking  
   - Core principles: empathy, definition, ideation, prototyping, testing  
   - Historical context in engineering and innovation education  

2. Empathy and User Research (20%) 
   - Observation techniques and ethnographic methods  
   - Conducting interviews and user journey mapping  
   - Identifying pain points and unmet needs  

3. Problem Definition and Framing (20%)
   - Structured problem statement development  
   - Using design thinking frameworks
   - Avoiding assumptions and bias in problem framing  

4. Ideation and Brainstorming (20%)
   - Techniques: mind mapping, SCAMPER, forced connections  
   - Constraints and creativity under limitations  
   - Evaluating idea feasibility and diversity  

5. Prototyping and Rapid Iteration  
   - Low-fidelity prototyping (paper, 3D printing, LEGO®-based)  
   - Iterative feedback loops and failure analysis  
   - Prototyping in real-world contexts (manufacturing environments)  

6. Testing and Validation  
   - User testing and feedback collection  
   - Metrics for success and usability  
   - Validating against sustainability and performance goals  

7. Sustainable Design Integration  
   - Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) basics and application  
   - Environmental and social impact evaluation  
   - Sustainable material and process selection  

8. Implementation and Scalability 
   - Transition from concept to real-world deployment  
   - Risk assessment and resource planning  
   - Industry alignment and scalability models  

9. Case Studies in Design Thinking 
   - Real-world manufacturing case studies  
   - Applying design thinking to existing industrial challenges  
   - Reflection and critique of outcomes  

10. Digital Tools and Environments  
   - Use of VR, simulation, and digital twins in prototyping  
   - Gamification and interactive learning (LCA game)  
   - Digital platforms for collaboration and feedback
