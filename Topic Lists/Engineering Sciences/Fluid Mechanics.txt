1. Fluid Statics (20%)
   - Pressure and hydrostatic forces in fluids
   - Fluid equilibrium and pressure variation with depth
   - Manometer systems and pressure measurement
   - Buoyancy and Archimedes’ principle
   - Hydrostatic forces on submerged surfaces

2. Fluid Dynamics (20%)
   - Conservation principles for mass, momentum, and energy
   - Continuity equation and fluid flow balance
   - Bernoulli’s equation and energy relationships in flow
   - Introduction to the Navier–Stokes equations
   - Flow classification including laminar and turbulent regimes
   - Reynolds number and flow regime characterization

3. Flow Measurement
   - Principles of fluid flow measurement
   - Orifice meters, venturi meters, and nozzles
   - Flow meter calibration and instrumentation
   - Measurement uncertainty and error analysis
   - Applications of flow measurement in industrial systems

4. Pipe Flow (20%)
   - Laminar and turbulent flow in pipes
   - Friction losses and pressure drop in pipelines
   - Darcy–Weisbach equation and head loss calculations
   - Use and interpretation of the Moody chart
   - Minor losses due to fittings and valves
   - Flow behavior in pipe networks

5. Compressible Flow
   - Fundamentals of compressible fluid behavior
   - Speed of sound and Mach number
   - Isentropic flow relations
   - Flow through converging and diverging nozzles
   - Normal and oblique shock waves

6. Turbulent Flow
   - Characteristics and scales of turbulence
   - Transition from laminar to turbulent flow
   - Energy dissipation in turbulent systems
   - Reynolds-averaged Navier–Stokes (RANS) equations
   - Turbulence modeling approaches including the k–ε model
   - Turbulence behavior in industrial applications

7. Fluid Machinery
   - Operation and classification of pumps, turbines, and fans
   - Performance curves and operating characteristics
   - Head, power, and efficiency relationships
   - Cavitation phenomena and prevention
   - Impeller design and system integration

8. Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD)
   - Introduction to computational modeling of fluid flow
   - Governing equations and numerical discretization
   - Mesh generation and computational domain setup
   - Boundary conditions and solver selection
   - Turbulence modeling in CFD simulations
   - Visualization and post-processing of simulation results

9. Applications in Manufacturing
   - Fluid systems used in manufacturing environments
   - Cooling and lubrication systems in production processes
   - Hydraulic and fluid power systems
   - Integration of fluid systems with automated manufacturing
   - Case studies of industrial fluid system design

10. Experimental Fluid Mechanics
   - Experimental design and laboratory methods
   - Flow visualization techniques
   - Pressure and velocity measurement instrumentation
   - Data acquisition and signal processing
   - Uncertainty analysis and experimental error propagation
