1. Atomic Structure and Bonding (15%)
   - Electron configuration and periodic trends
   - Ionic, covalent, and metallic bonding
   - Crystal structures (FCC, BCC, and HCP)
   - Polymer and ceramic microstructures
   - Defects (point, line, planar, and volume defects)

2. Phase Equilibria and Diagrams (15%)
   - Binary and ternary phase diagrams (Fe–Fe3C, Cu–Ni, and Al–Si)
   - Weldability and critical cooling curves
   - Solidification processes
   - Eutectic and peritectic reactions

3. Mechanical Behavior of Materials (15%)
   - Stress–strain relationships
   - Yield strength, ductility, and toughness
   - Elasticity and plasticity
   - Viscoelasticity, creep, and fatigue
   - Low-cycle and high-cycle fatigue behavior

4. Failure Analysis and Critical Thinking (15%)
   - Fracture mechanics
   - Root cause analysis
   - Failure criteria including leak-before-break and proof testing
   - Failure case studies and failure-based learning

5. Thermal and Environmental Effects
   - Thermal expansion and thermal stress
   - Thermal stability of materials
   - Corrosion mechanisms
   - Corrosion prevention techniques

6. Processing and Microstructure Development
   - Manufacturing processes (casting, forging, extrusion, and rolling)
   - Heat treatment processes (annealing, quenching, and tempering)
   - Surface hardening methods (carburizing and nitriding)
   - Shot peening and microstructure modification

7. Computational and Simulation Tools
   - Finite element methods (FEM)
   - Discrete element methods (DEM)
   - Molecular dynamics simulations
   - Simulation of micro- and nano-scale material behavior

8. Sustainable and Green Materials Design
   - Life cycle assessment of materials
   - Recyclability and environmental impact
   - Principles of sustainable materials design
   - Green manufacturing practices
