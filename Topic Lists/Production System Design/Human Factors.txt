1. Human-Centered Design Principles (20%)
   - Cognitive load and mental models  
   - User-centered design (UCD) workflows  
   - Design for usability and accessibility  
   - Inclusive design and diversity in human performance  

2. Human Performance and Error Analysis  
   - Error types: slips, lapses, mistakes, violations  
   - Human reliability and failure modes  
   - Cognitive and physical limitations under stress  
   - Decision-making under uncertainty  

3. Ergonomics and Physical Interaction (20%)
   - Postural stress and musculoskeletal disorders  
   - Workstation design and reach analysis  
   - Thermal and environmental comfort  
   - Fatigue modeling and work-rest cycles  

4. Human-System Interface Design (20%)
   - Interface clarity and information hierarchy  
   - Visual and auditory feedback design including AR and VR
   - Haptic and multimodal feedback  
   - Interface consistency and learnability  

5. Cognitive Workload and Task Design  
   - Task decomposition and cognitive load theory  
   - Workload assessment tools (NASA-TLX)  
   - Task sequencing and workflow optimization  
   - Decision support systems and automation boundaries  

6. Safety and Risk Management in Human-System Interaction  
   - Hazard identification and risk assessment  
   - Safety-critical interface design  
   - Emergency response procedures and human factors  
   - Human error mitigation strategies  

7. Training, Skill Development, and Human Performance Improvement  
   - Skill acquisition models (skill acquisition curve)  
   - Feedback loops and performance monitoring  
   - Simulation-based training environments  
   - Maintenance and operational training in learning factories  

8. Human Factors in Automation and Cyber-Physical Systems  
   - Human-automation interaction (HAI)  
   - Trust and confidence in automated systems 
   - Ethical and practical considerations (Future of Work)
   - Data privacy and security in vision systems  
   - Maintenance, calibration, and system reliability 
   - Human-in-the-loop (HITL) design principles  
   - System transparency and explainability   

9. Evaluation and Validation of Human Factors Design  
   - Usability testing and field trials  
   - Iterative design and prototyping  
   - Metrics for human performance and satisfaction
