1. Foundations of Inspection and Testing  
   - Definition of inspection vs. testing  
   - Types of inspection: attribute vs. variable  
   - Sampling plans (AQL, OC curves)  
   - Sampling strategies: random, systematic, stratified  

2. Statistical Process Control (SPC) Principles  
   - Control charts: X-bar and R charts  
   - Process stability and capability (Cp, Cpk)  
   - Common and special cause variation  
   - Data collection protocols and measurement systems analysis (MSA)  

3. Measurement and Calibration  
   - Tool selection (rulers, protractors, digital calipers)  
   - Calibration procedures and traceability  
   - Measurement uncertainty and repeatability  

4. Design of Inspection Procedures  
   - Flowchart development for inspection tasks  
   - Standardized measurement points (top edge, diagonal)  
   - Sample selection logic (fixed four squares per group)  
   - Role assignment in team-based inspection  

5. Validation of Test Methods  
   - Test method verification (does it meet specifications?)  
   - Validation against design requirements
   - Error analysis: random vs. non-random variation  

6. Process Capability Analysis  
   - Calculation of Cp and Cpk  
   - Interpretation of capability indices  
   - Comparison of process performance to specification limits  

7. Feedback Loops and Continuous Improvement  
   - PDCA cycle application (Plan-Do-Check-Act)  
   - Identifying root causes of variation  
   - Implementation of corrective actions  

8. Tools and Technology Integration  
   - Use of Excel for control chart generation  
   - Digital tools for real-time data entry and monitoring  
   - Jig design and application in consistent production  

9. Quality Management Frameworks  
   - Integration of QFD (Quality Function Deployment)  
   - Role of quality in engineering design decisions  

10. Ethical and Practical Considerations  
   - Accuracy of measurements under real-world conditions  
   - Documentation and traceability of inspection records
