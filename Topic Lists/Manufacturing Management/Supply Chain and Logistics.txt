1. Supply Chain Fundamentals (20%)
   - Structure of supply chains (raw materials to end customer)  
   - Key actors (suppliers, manufacturers, distributors, retailers)  
   - Flow of goods, information, and finances  
   - Supply chain types (make-to-stock, make-to-order, assemble-to-order)  
   - Supply chain lifecycle (design, planning, execution, control)  

2. Demand Forecasting and Planning (20%) 
   - Time-series and causal forecasting methods  
   - Moving averages, exponential smoothing, ARIMA  
   - Demand signal processing and lead time estimation  
   - Safety stock calculation and service level targets  

3. Inventory Management  (20%)
   - ABC analysis and EOQ model  
   - Just-in-time (JIT) vs. safety stock strategies  
   - Inventory turnover and carrying cost analysis  
   - Multi-echelon inventory systems  

4. Logistics Operations  
   - Transportation modes (road, rail, air, sea)  
   - Route optimization and freight cost modeling  
   - Warehouse layout and picking strategies  
   - Order fulfillment and last-mile delivery  

5. Supply Chain Design and Network Optimization  
   - Facility location models (gravity, p-median)  
   - Network design under uncertainty  
   - Capacity planning and bottleneck analysis  

6. Supply Chain Risk Management  
   - Risk identification (disruptions, supplier failure)  
   - Contingency planning and resilience strategies  
   - Scenario analysis and stress testing  

7. Quality Management in Supply Chains  
   - Statistical Process Control (SPC) – X-bar and R charts  
   - Root cause analysis (5 Whys, Fishbone)  
   - Quality metrics (Cpk, Ppk, defect rate)  
   - Continuous improvement (PDCA, Kaizen)  

8. Sustainability and Ethical Supply Chains  
   - Environmental impact assessment (carbon footprint)  
   - Ethical sourcing and labor practices  
   - Circular economy and waste reduction  

9. Technology Integration  
   - ERP and SCM software (SAP, Oracle)  
   - IoT and real-time tracking  
   - Digital twins and simulation tools  

10. Project Management in Supply Chains  
   - Gantt charts, critical path method (CPM)  
   - Scope, time, cost, and risk management  
   - Stakeholder communication  

11. Experiential Learning & Simulation  
   - Hands-on manufacturing exercises (Manufacturing Squares)  
   - Business simulation games (supply chain games)  
   - SAP-based logistics labs  

12. Ethics and Decision-Making in Supply Chains  
   - Ethical dilemmas in sourcing and distribution  
   - Stakeholder equity and transparency
